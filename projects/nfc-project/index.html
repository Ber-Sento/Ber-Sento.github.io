Embedded NFC Systems in Additively Manufactured Structures

Integration of Near Field Communication technology into custom 3D printed products for interactive information sharing and product presentation.

May 2026
  • 3D Printing
  • NFC
  • Embedded Systems
  • Product Design

Project Overview

This project explores the integration of Near Field Communication (NFC) technology within additively manufactured structures. The objective was to create functional 3D printed objects capable of storing and sharing digital information through a simple smartphone tap while maintaining structural integrity and reliable signal transmission.

Personal Motivation

I am particularly interested in how technology can create intuitive interactions between people and engineered systems. Although this project does not involve robotics directly, it explores the broader concept of connecting physical objects with digital information through simple and natural user interaction.

The project provided an opportunity to combine additive manufacturing, product design, and embedded technology while investigating practical methods for enhancing user experience. These concepts are closely related to my long-term interests in robotics, human-robot interaction, and the development of systems that integrate seamlessly into everyday life.

Objectives

  • Embed NFC tags within custom 3D printed products.
  • Evaluate NFC signal transmission through PLA structures.
  • Investigate the influence of wall thickness and air gaps.
  • Create practical objects for digital information sharing.
  • Develop aesthetically integrated NFC-enabled products.

Design and Manufacturing Process

CAD Design

Models were developed in Fusion 360 with dedicated internal cavities designed according to NTAG dimensions. Multiple geometries were explored to optimize tag placement while preserving structural strength.

Additive Manufacturing

Parts were fabricated using FDM 3D printing with PLA material. Several print configurations were evaluated to determine optimal NFC readability and product appearance.

Assembly

NFC tags were embedded into the printed structures during assembly, creating a seamless integration between digital functionality and physical product design.

Engineering Challenges

NFC Readability Through Plastic

The thickness of printed material directly affects smartphone communication with the embedded NFC antenna. Different cover thicknesses were evaluated to determine practical design limits.

Air Gap Optimization

Experiments were conducted to understand how spacing around the NFC tag influences signal performance and reading consistency.

Tag Placement

Several tag orientations were tested to maximize compatibility across different smartphone models.

Results
